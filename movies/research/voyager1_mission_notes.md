# Voyager 1 Mission Notes

Primary sources used for the educational reel:

- NASA mission overview: `https://science.nasa.gov/mission/voyager/voyager-1/`
- NASA mission status: `https://science.nasa.gov/mission/voyager/where-are-voyager-1-and-voyager-2-now/`
- NASA update published April 17, 2026:
  `https://science.nasa.gov/blogs/mission-voyager/2026/04/17/a-pause-all-well-and-new-resources/`

Facts used in captions:

- Launch date: September 5, 1977.
- Jupiter closest approach: March 5, 1979.
- Saturn closest approach: November 12, 1980.
- Interstellar crossing: August 2012.
- April 17, 2026 update: Voyager 1 is more than 15 billion miles from Earth,
  more than 25 billion kilometers away, and radio signals take about 23 hours
  one way.

Derived value:

- `25 billion km / 149,597,870.7 km per AU` is about `167 AU`.
